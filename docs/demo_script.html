段落 / 時間English (spoken)中文對照
1. 問題0:00–0:40 Every carbon-removal project hits the same wall: measurement. How much carbon does a plant actually hold? Today, the only way to know is to cut it down and oven-dry it for days. It kills the plant, and it's far too slow and costly to repeat. So even projects that truly capture carbon often can't prove how much — and without proof, there's no credit. 畫面：簡報 Slide 2（Problem）。標題卡 + 「harvest → oven-dry 2–3 days → destroyed」的破壞性流程示意。 每個碳移除專案都會撞上同一道牆：量測。一株植物到底固了多少碳？今天唯一的辦法，是把它砍下來、烘乾好幾天。會毀掉植株，又慢又貴、無法大量重複。於是就算真的固了碳，也常常證明不了多少——沒有證據，就沒有碳權。
2. 構想0:40–1:10 LeafCarbon replaces that with a single photo. You set up a simple stand, take one picture of a plant, and the model estimates its dry weight — and from that, its carbon — each with a confidence range. Non-destructive, and a few seconds instead of days. 畫面：簡報 Slide 3（The idea）。固定拍攝架（側拍）→ 一張照片 → 模型輸出（乾重 → 碳）。 LeafCarbon 把它換成一張照片。架個簡單的支架、拍一株植物，模型就估出乾重、再換算成碳量，每個都附一個信賴範圍。不破壞植株，幾秒就好、不用好幾天。
3. 實機 Demo1:10–2:40 Here's the tool. I pick one lettuce photo. (① input) The model finds the green leaves and boxes them, (② detected canopy) then reads a few plain numbers off that shape — height, width-to-height, leaf density, greenness — plus the weather it grew in; it's all on screen, no black box. (③ model inputs) From those it gives a dry weight with a confidence range, (④ dry weight) and carbon is simply that × 0.47. (⑤ carbon) These are four different plants at different ages — day 7, 20, 27, 35 — the older the plant, the higher the estimate. (step through the four thumbnails) 畫面：切到互動 Demo 實機錄影，照畫面 ①→⑤ 的順序講、講到哪指到哪；最後依序點四個 Day 縮圖。注意：各天是不同植株（逐日烘乾取真值），不是同一株的時間序。 這就是工具。我選一張萵苣照片。(①輸入照片) 模型找出綠葉、框起來，(②偵測冠層) 再從形狀讀出幾個直白的量——株高、寬高比、葉子密度、綠的多寡——加上它生長時的環境；全顯示在畫面上，沒有黑盒。(③模型輸入) 據此給出乾重和信賴範圍，(④乾重) 碳量就是它 ×0.47。(⑤碳量) 這四張是不同年齡的不同植株——第 7、20、27、35 天——植株愈老、估值愈高。(依序點四個縮圖)
4. 碳的換算2:40–3:15 Carbon comes straight from biomass. Dry plant matter is almost a fixed fraction carbon — about forty-seven percent, and across four thousand species it barely moves. So carbon is just dry weight times zero-point-four-seven. We only have to get the weight right — no chemistry on each plant. We chose lettuce on purpose: a thirty-five-day cycle lets us test the method fast. 畫面：簡報 Slide 5（Carbon = biomass × 0.47）。等式大字 + IPCC 2006 / Ma et al. 2018 來源小字 + 「lettuce = fast validation crop, 35-day」標註。 碳量直接由生物量換算。乾掉的植物幾乎是固定比例的碳——約 47%，四千多個物種裡幾乎不變。所以碳就是乾重 × 0.47。我們只要把重量量準，不必對每株做化學分析。刻意挑萵苣：35 天的週期讓我們能很快驗證方法。
5. 成績與成本3:15–4:05 How well does it work? On our own dataset — seventy-two lettuce plants over twenty-four days — the estimate lands close to the real oven-dried weight, an R-squared around zero-point-six-four. And we tested it the honest way: the model is always scored on a day it has never seen. This is a measured result, not a simulation. The win is the contrast — days and a destroyed plant become one photo and a few seconds — and because nothing is harvested, you can photograph the same plant again tomorrow. 畫面：簡報 Slide 6（Results）。三個大數字 R²≈0.64 / RMSE≈0.43 g / 留一日（72 株·24 天）；右側「days + destroyed」對「seconds + repeatable」對比；可疊一段 Demo 的信賴範圍特寫。 效果如何？在我們自己的資料集上——72 株萵苣、橫跨 24 天——估計值很接近真實的烘乾重量，R² 約 0.64。而且用誠實的方式測：模型每次都被拿沒看過的一天來打分。這是實測，不是模擬。價值在對比——好幾天加一株被毀的植株，變成一張照片、幾秒——而且因為不採收，同一株明天還能再拍一次。
6. 邊界與收尾4:05–4:35 One honest boundary: this is a measurement, not a carbon credit — and lettuce is just our test crop, not the carbon sink. What we've built is a cheap, non-destructive ruler. The same ruler can move to the pools that really matter — forests, soils, restoration land — where cheap measurement is exactly what's missing today. 畫面：簡報 Slide 7（Scope）→ Slide 8（Close）。先打出「measurement ≠ credit · lettuce ≠ sink」誠實邊界，再切森林／土壤／復育場景，LeafCarbon 標誌定格。 一個誠實的邊界：這是量測，不是碳權；萵苣只是測試作物，不是碳匯標的。我們做的是一把便宜、非破壞的尺。這把尺可以移到真正重要的碳庫——森林、土壤、復育地——而那裡今天最缺的，正是便宜的量測。
