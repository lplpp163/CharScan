The bottleneck

For carbon removal,
the hard part is measurement.

harvest plant→ oven-dry 2–3 days→ plant destroyed
Too slow and costly to repeat at scale → projects can't prove how much carbon → can't earn credit.
The idea

A measurement
that is just a photo.

One fixed-setup photo → a model estimates dry biomass, then carbon — each with a confidence range.

setup
Fixed side-view rig
Same distance & angle every time — phone camera is enough.
calibrate · once
A few oven-dried samples
Anchors “image → biomass”. After that, every new plant is only a photo.

From biomass to carbon
carbon=dry biomass×0.47

The carbon fraction of dry plant matter is nearly constant — so we only need to measure biomass well. No per-plant elemental analysis.

0.47 — IPCC 2006 default carbon fraction · Ma et al. 2018: 45.0–47.9% across 4,318 species
Lettuce — a fast validation crop, chosen on purpose: a 35-day cycle tunes the method quickly. Not the carbon sink.
